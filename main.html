Manali


Manali is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Kullu Valley of Himachal Pradesh, India, situated at an altitude of 2,050 meters (6,730 feet). Known as the "Valley of the Gods", it is celebrated for its breathtaking natural beauty, snow-capped mountains, lush forests, and serene rivers like the Beas. The town is deeply rooted in Hindu mythology, with its name derived from "Manu-Alaya", meaning the "abode of Manu", the progenitor of humanity, who, according to legend, survived a great flood and resettled here.

Top 5 attaraction

  1. Uluwatu Temple - A stunning sea temple perched on a cliff.
  2. Rice Terraces - Beautifully terraced fields showcasing Bali's agricultural heritage.
  3. Mount Batur - A popular spot for sunrise hikes with breathtaking views.
  4. Seminyak Beach - Known for its upscale beach clubs and vibrant nightlife.
  5. Monkey Forest - A sanctuary for the playful long-tailed macaques.

Must try food

  • Nasi Goreng - A flavorful fried rice dish, often served with a fried egg.
  • Sate Lilit - Minced meat satay wrapped around lemongrass sticks.
  • Bebek Betutu - Slow-cooked duck stuffed with spices and wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Gado-Gado - A mixed vegetable salad with a rich peanut sauce.
  • Ayam Betutu - Spicy roasted chicken with traditional Balinese spices.
